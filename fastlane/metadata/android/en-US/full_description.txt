Field Diagnose turns the readings off your analog gauges, multimeter, and a line thermometer into a real HVAC diagnosis — refrigerant charge, airflow, electrical, and delivered capacity. No account, no cloud, no Bluetooth probes, no subscription.

The diagnostic apps technicians know are proprietary, online, and often tied to a hardware ecosystem. Field Diagnose is the open alternative for the tech with basic tools and the under-resourced shop — it does the math a digital manifold does, from gauges you already own.

Features:

* Charge: a 23-refrigerant pressure-temperature table, superheat/subcool vs target; for fixed-orifice systems the target superheat is computed from indoor wet-bulb + outdoor dry-bulb.
* Air-side: Delta-T, psychrometrics (wet-bulb, dew point, enthalpy, grains), and delivered capacity — sensible/latent/total BTU, SHR, and % of nominal.
* Electrical: running amps vs nameplate RLA/LRA, run-capacitor microfarads, locked-rotor, plus 13 guided multimeter troubleshooting sequences.
* Condenser split, a live P-T chart, a 0–100 system health score, and a shareable text/PDF report.
* Optional: a GPS to NOAA (api.weather.gov) button that auto-fills the outdoor-air temperature. This is the only network feature, it is opt-in, and the diagnosis itself is fully offline.

How it works offline: refrigerant saturation physics is precomputed into a static table the app interpolates, so the phone needs no heavy property library — the whole engine runs on-device.

For qualified technicians. This is a diagnostic aid, not a substitute for training or for following equipment and safety procedures.
